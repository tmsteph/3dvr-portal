Fascia Release

Here is a gentle fascia-release reset for neck, jaw, tongue, and computer tension. Keep everything at 2-4/10 intensity. No forcing, cracking, aggressive stretching, or deep pressure on the front of the throat.

"Tongue soft, jaw heavy, eyes soft."

10-minute neck/jaw fascia release

Move slowly. Stay small. Keep the whole routine calm.

The sequence below follows the original reset: downshift first, then jaw, SCM, suboccipitals, chest and collarbone, and a final chin-tuck decompression.

0190 seconds

Downshift first

Sit or lie down. One hand on chest, one on belly. Inhale 4 seconds, exhale 6-8 seconds. On each exhale, quietly say, "tongue soft, jaw heavy, eyes soft."

  • Use nose breathing if possible.
  • Let the exhale be longer than the inhale.
  • Drop the clench before anything else.
022 minutes

Tongue and jaw reset

Place the tongue gently on the roof of the mouth behind the upper front teeth. Set the jaw to tongue up, teeth apart, lips lightly closed, jaw hanging.

  • Do 5 tiny jaw openings, only 1-2 finger widths.
  • Massage the cheek muscle and jaw angle with small circles.
  • Keep pressure light and avoid digging into the joint.
032 minutes

SCM release

Turn the head slightly left. On the right side, gently pinch the sternocleidomastoid between fingers, not the throat, and glide downward toward the collarbone.

  • Use 3-5 slow passes per side.
  • Pressure stays gentle; do not dig.
  • This area is sensitive because of nearby nerves and vessels.
042 minutes

Suboccipital release

Lie on your back. Put two tennis balls in a sock, or use fingertips, under the base of the skull. Let the head rest without rolling aggressively.

  • Make tiny yes nods for 30 seconds.
  • Make tiny no motions for 30 seconds.
  • Then just breathe and melt for 60 seconds.
0590 seconds

Pec and collarbone sweep

Gently sweep under the collarbone from sternum outward to shoulder, then massage the pec area below the collarbone.

  • Spend 45 seconds each side.
  • Let the shoulder stay relaxed.
  • This often matters more than the neck alone.
061 minute

Chin tuck decompression

Sit tall, imagine the back of the skull floating upward, then slide the head backward like making a small double chin. Do not look down.

  • Hold 3 seconds, then release.
  • Do 5-8 reps.
  • Keep discomfort very low, ideally 0-3/10.

The computer jaw rule

Use this 15-second reset every 20-30 minutes.

"Feet grounded. Exhale. Tongue up. Teeth apart. Shoulders drop. Chin gently back. Eyes look far away."

Why it helps

Computer work often recreates the same clench and forward-head pattern. A short reset can interrupt that loop before it becomes the whole day.

Keep it tiny

The point is not to fix the body in one move. The point is to lower effort enough that the jaw and neck stop bracing.

What I would avoid

Stay away from hard, corrective, or aggressive work.

Do not force the neck

Skip hard cracking, aggressive stretching, and deep pressure on the front of the throat.

Do not force the jaw

If the jaw clicks, locks, hurts, or the bite changes, stop trying to expand or align it yourself.

Use care with tools

Aggressive gua sha or strong pressure over the throat is not part of this reset.

Get help when needed

A dentist or TMJ-aware physical therapist is worth it if the symptoms persist or the bite feels changed.
