Downshift first

Sit or lie down. One hand on chest, one on belly. Inhale 4 seconds, exhale 6-8 seconds. On each exhale, silently say, "tongue soft, jaw heavy, eyes soft."

  • Use nose breathing if possible.
  • Let the exhale be longer than the inhale.
  • Drop the clench before anything else.

The computer jaw rule

Use this 15-second reset every 20-30 minutes.

Feet grounded. Exhale. Tongue up. Teeth apart. Shoulders drop. Chin gently back. Eyes look far away.

Why it matters

Computer work recreates clench, shallow breathing, and forward head posture. A short reset interrupts the loop.

Keep it tiny

The point is not to fix the body in one move. The point is to lower effort enough that the jaw and neck stop bracing.

What to avoid

  • Hard neck cracking.
  • Aggressive stretching or deep throat pressure.
  • Forcing jaw alignment or trying to expand the jaw yourself.

When to stop

  • Jaw clicking, locking, or pain increases.
  • The bite feels changed.
  • Dizziness, numbness, weakness, or severe pain appears.

Practical note

A dentist or TMJ-aware physical therapist is worth it if the symptoms persist or the bite feels changed.
