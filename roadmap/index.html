3dvr.tech roadmap

March 2026 to February 2027

Build a simple revenue engine without dropping the mission.

This roadmap turns 3dvr.tech into a practical service business first, then grows it into community software and a marketplace. The goal is profitability inside 12 months while keeping the core open-source and empowerment-first.

$20/month offer 50 members = $1,000 MRR proof Community + tools + marketplace

Core offer

Web Presence for Builders

WordPress + tech friend + startup incubator.

  • Simple website setup and hosting
  • Routine updates and tech support
  • Help launching projects and offers
  • Low-friction pricing for people who dislike managing technology

Mission guardrails

Charge for execution, not lock-in.

3dvr can stay aligned with its open-source mission if revenue comes from hosting, support, launch help, and convenience while the shared builder knowledge keeps getting stronger.

Keep the core open

Publish templates, playbooks, and reusable builder tools where practical so customers are buying momentum, not a closed platform.

Turn service into leverage

Every repeated request should become a documented process, a shared template, or a future product feature for the community.

Make customers part of the network

Treat subscribers as builders with access to shared support, intros, and collaboration instead of as passive agency clients.

Sell independence

The message is bigger than web services: 3dvr helps people build their dreams without being trapped by expensive agencies or opaque tools.

Execution plan

Five phases from service proof to platform leverage.

Start with one clear offer, prove that it converts, then use real customer demand to decide which tools and network features deserve product time.

Phase 1 | March-April 2026

One clear offer

Strip the pitch down to a single monthly service ordinary builders can understand fast.

  • Package the $20 plan around websites, hosting, updates, and launch help
  • Use simple positioning and one repeatable onboarding flow
  • Stop splitting attention across too many unrelated offers

Success metric: five early members and a clean onboarding checklist.

Phase 2 | May-June 2026

Get the first 50 paying members

Prove the business by selling to people who already trust your technical judgment.

  • Reach out to AV professionals, artists, nonprofits, DJs, teachers, and local businesses
  • Use direct messages, short calls, and practical demos instead of broad cold marketing
  • Capture objections, launch blockers, and simple wins from every customer conversation

Success metric: 50 members and $1,000 in monthly recurring revenue.

Phase 3 | July-August 2026

Turn customers into community

Make the subscription feel like belonging to a builder network, not renting a brochure site.

  • Create a builder chat, shared knowledge base, and simple support rituals
  • Introduce collaborations, referrals, and public customer wins
  • Build lightweight tutorials from real member questions

Success metric: measurable retention and member-to-member referrals.

Phase 4 | September-November 2026

Productize repeated requests

Only build software after the first wave of customers shows what keeps slowing them down.

  • Prioritize a website generator, notes, CRM, project boards, or hosting controls only if demand repeats
  • Bundle the best internal tools into the subscription
  • Keep Gun-powered collaboration as the shared source of truth across the builder workflow

Success metric: one or two tools that reduce support time or speed up launches.

Phase 5 | December 2026-February 2027

Add a marketplace layer

Let the network trade services so 3dvr earns from coordination as well as subscriptions.

  • Enable members to offer design, marketing, consulting, hosting, and development help
  • Take a small transaction fee or introduce a premium membership tier
  • Use the marketplace to reinforce community value instead of replacing it

Success metric: subscriptions plus marketplace revenue from active member deals.

Revenue math

Simple enough to track every month.

The first target is not maximum revenue. It is proof that the offer works well enough to repeat.

Best-fit customers

Start with the people already in reach.

  • AV professionals and event planners
  • Artists, DJs, creators, and freelancers
  • Teachers, nonprofits, and small local businesses
  • Anyone who needs a site but does not want to manage technology
"I run a small builder community where we help people launch websites and projects. For $20/month we host and help maintain your site and help you build things."

This week

Do the boring, repeatable work first.

  1. Refine the $20 offer until one sentence explains it clearly.
  2. Reach out to 20 people already inside your network.
  3. Get five early members and onboard them by hand.
  4. Help those members succeed fast enough to create trust and proof.
  5. Document their wins and turn those stories into the next wave of marketing.

Founder focus

The main risk is fragmentation, not capability.

3dvr already has technical depth, open-source credibility, industry experience, and a community mindset. The hard part is resisting the urge to build five futures at once.

  • Invent less and sell more in the first 90 days
  • Use customer wins to decide what deserves product time
  • Keep repeating the message: we help people build their dreams
  • Let service revenue finance the bigger ecosystem vision

Drafted March 12, 2026. The sequence is simple: service proof, builder community, shared tools, then a marketplace.
