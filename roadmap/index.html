Portal loop

Keep everything tied to the same working system.

1. Collect signal

Start in the worksheet.

List reviewers, objections, questions, and decisions while the wording is still fresh.

2. Add structure

Create groups and people in CRM.

Groups hold accounts. People sit under groups. Use the quick lead lane when speed matters.

3. Link the pain

Track problems once.

Log each repeated pain as its own CRM problem record and link it back to the right people and groups.

4. Work the next step

Push strong records into action.

Use follow-up dates, touch logs, and shared contacts to keep promising leads moving.

Market-fit rules

Stay close to real people, repeated pain, and pilot speed.

Who

Use warm reach first.

Owner-led teams, local operators, creatives, educators, and existing relationships stay at the front of the queue.

Pain

Sell relief, not platform theory.

Unclear offer, weak homepage, scattered contacts, and missed follow-up are the pains worth tracking first.

Pilot

Spend time where action can happen now.

The strongest records have trust, active work, and a next step that can happen this month.
