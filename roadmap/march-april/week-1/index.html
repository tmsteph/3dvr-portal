Daily plan

Seven days.

Thursday, March 19

Kick off the campaign.

  • Show the landing page and the current Free, $5/mo, and $20/mo ladder.
  • Ask people where the offer feels unclear, weak, or incomplete.
  • Leave with a real list of questions instead of defending the current copy.

Friday, March 20

Sort the first reactions.

  • Move every question into buckets: price, offer, trust, proof, and next step.
  • Highlight the wording that repeats.
  • Cut duplicate notes down to the clearest version.

Saturday, March 21

Tighten the message.

  • Rewrite the one-sentence pitch three different ways.
  • Test which version feels easiest to say and easiest to understand.
  • Keep only the language that makes the ladder clearer.

Sunday, March 22

Clean up the decision list.

  • Separate true decisions from open questions.
  • Write one sentence for what must be locked on Thursday.
  • Delete anything that can wait until after launch.

Monday, March 23

Get outside signal.

  • Put the current ladder in front of a few warm contacts who were not in the kickoff.
  • Ask where they hesitate, not whether they like it.
  • Capture the phrases that lead to the cleanest response.

Tuesday, March 24

Turn notes into choices.

  • Write the top two or three ladder options still worth discussing.
  • Draft the strongest version of each option.
  • Prepare the tradeoffs so Thursday does not drift.

Wednesday, March 25

Prep the Thursday close.

  • Bring one short summary, one decision list, and one current pitch.
  • Put the repeated objections at the top.
  • Show only the choices that are ready to approve.
