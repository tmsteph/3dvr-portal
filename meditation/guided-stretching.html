Stretch routine overview

Move slowly, stay aware of your range, and keep every motion smooth. Hold each position for the suggested time or longer if your body invites it. Breathe evenly through the nose throughout.

  1. Phase 1 • 3 minutes

    Awaken and align

    Start standing. Roll shoulders back, interlace fingers overhead for a long reach, then side bend right and left. Finish with slow neck circles and ankle rolls.

  2. Phase 2 • 4 minutes

    Dynamic flow

    Step into alternating forward lunges, dropping the back knee gently. Add an overhead reach, then sweep arms down to frame the front foot, easing into a runner's stretch.

  3. Phase 3 • 5 minutes

    Grounded release

    Come to the floor. Sit tall for a seated twist on each side, move into a figure-four stretch, then finish with a supported child's pose for at least eight breaths.

Breath cues for each pose

  • Inhale to lengthen the spine and create space, exhale to settle deeper without strain.
  • Keep the jaw relaxed and let the shoulders drop away from the ears on every exhale.
  • Match the cadence from the breathing room or slow to a calm four-count wave if you prefer.

Stay within comfort

If a shape causes sharp sensation, ease back or choose a gentler variation. Micro-movements and breath-led sways are just as powerful as deep holds.

Pair with breaks

Use the flow during mid-day resets. Set a soft chime for twelve minutes, complete the sequence, then hydrate and take a short walk to integrate the release.

Extend the practice

After the final child's pose, transition straight into a seated meditation or journaling moment while your body is relaxed and your breath stays steady.

Listen inward, honor your edge, and let each stretch be an invitation rather than a demand.
