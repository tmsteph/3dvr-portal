The phone needs a nervous system.

Current focus modes mostly ask people to maintain static allow and block lists. Attention Shield starts from a different assumption: interruption is expensive, and the device should defend the human by default.

Humans before feeds

Real people and time-sensitive work can pass through. Engagement bait, vanity metrics, and sales pressure get delayed.

Context before rules

Deep work, driving, sleep, recovery, and family time need different thresholds without rebuilding every app setting.

Local first

The preference graph should live with the user. Cloud AI can be optional later, not the default gatekeeper.

Modes that understand the point.

Deep Work

Allow direct calls, important contacts, calendar changes, and critical business messages. Everything else becomes an hourly digest.

Human Mode

Favor actual people and suppress algorithmic noise, social vanity metrics, flash sales, and habit-loop notifications.

Nature / Yoga Mode

Emergency-only interruptions, grayscale-friendly UI, gentle sound defaults, and a low-stimulation notification surface.

  • Sender importance

3. Portal dashboard

  • Digest history
  • Contact priority graph

Why it belongs in 3DVR

The product is not another assistant. It is an AI protection layer for sensory-friendly, open, user-owned computing.
