Launch path

One room, existing portal tools

The Launch Room does not replace the portal modules. It gives builders a clear order for using them.

1 Shape the project Use Projects to name the idea, owner, audience, and first shippable outcome. 2 Make the page Use Website Builder / Sites to draft, preview, revise, and publish the public page. 3 Capture demand Use Lead Capture to turn interest into form submissions the team can follow up on. 4 Open payment Use Billing to connect the offer to plans, subscriptions, and customer status.
Module map

How the portal connects

Each module keeps doing its own job. Launch Room explains when to use it and links straight there.

Projects Turns the rough idea into a visible project with context, next steps, and ownership.
Website Builder / Sites Creates the first public page so the idea has a real destination to share. Open Web Builder Open Sites
Lead Capture Collects names, emails, and notes from people who want to hear more. CRM Tracks the pipeline after a lead becomes a conversation, opportunity, or deal. Contacts Keeps people, companies, tags, and relationship context reusable across launches. Tasks Breaks the launch into concrete work: page edits, follow-ups, pricing, and handoff items. Billing Gives the project a payment path through plans, checkout, and subscription status. Email Operator Turns approvals, replies, and follow-ups into practical outreach without duplicating CRM work. Sales Provides launch scripts, lead review, buyer steps, and revenue playbooks.

What leaves the room

  • A project record with the idea, audience, and next move.
  • A launch page or site draft ready to improve.
  • A lead path connected to CRM and Contacts.
  • A billing path for subscriptions, setup work, or support.

Use it when

You have an idea that is still too vague to sell, but ready enough to turn into a page, a follow-up list, and a practical path toward payment.
